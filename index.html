Should I unplug chargers when they are not in use?

Many small electronic devices such as phones and tablets have chargers. Even when not in use, the chargers still consumes some electricity. The power consumption of most chargers manufactured in recent years is negligible when not in use, and unplugging them produces no meaningful power savings.

The easiest way to tell whether you should unplug a particular charger is simply touch it with you hand after it's plugged in for a hour without actively charging. If you can feel the warmth, unplug it will have meaningful power savings. If it's cold to the touch, you can simply leave it plugged in.
